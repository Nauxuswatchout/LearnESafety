Internet Guidelines

Protecting Personal Information

  • Keep private information private

    Never share your full name, address, phone number, school name, or financial information online, especially in public forums or with people you don't know personally.

  • Use strong, unique passwords

    Create complex passwords that include a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Use different passwords for different accounts and change them regularly.

  • Enable two-factor authentication

    When available, enable two-factor authentication for an extra layer of security on important accounts.

Family Online Safety Measures

  • Set up parental controls

    Use built-in parental controls on devices, browsers, and applications to filter inappropriate content and limit screen time.

  • Keep devices in common areas

    Encourage the use of computers, tablets, and other devices in shared family spaces rather than in bedrooms.

  • Establish clear rules

    Create and discuss family internet rules, including time limits, appropriate websites and apps, and consequences for breaking rules.

Safe Online Communication

  • Be cautious with new online friends

    Remember that people online may not be who they claim to be. Never meet someone in person whom you've only met online without parent supervision.

  • Think before posting

    Once something is posted online, it can be difficult or impossible to remove. Consider how posts might affect your reputation or feelings of others.

  • Report inappropriate behavior

    If someone is making you uncomfortable online, tell a trusted adult and report the behavior to the platform.

Recognise and Avoid Online Scams

  • Be wary of "too good to be true" offers

    If something seems too good to be true, it probably is. Be skeptical of free giveaways, prizes, or incredible deals.

  • Check before clicking

    Hover over links to see where they actually lead before clicking. Don't open attachments or click links in emails from unknown senders.

  • Keep software updated

    Regularly update your operating system, browsers, and apps to protect against security vulnerabilities.
