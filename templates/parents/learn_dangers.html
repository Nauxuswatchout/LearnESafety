Learn About Online Dangers

The internet can be confusing, but you're not alone.

This section is here to help you understand the online risks that affect both you and your children.

Cyberbullying

Cyberbullying

Bullying that takes place over digital devices like cell phones, computers, and tablets through SMS, text, apps, online games, or social media platforms.

Learn More

Online Predators

Online Predators

Individuals who use the internet to identify and groom potential victims, typically through flattery, sympathy, and by offering understanding or gifts.

Learn More

Phishing & Scams

Phishing & Scams

Deceptive attempts to steal personal information like usernames, passwords, location and credit card details by disguising as trustworthy entities.

Learn More

Inappropriate Content

Inappropriate Content

Material not suitable for children including violent content, pornography, hate speech, drug use, and other adult themes that children may stumble upon.

Learn More

Virus & Malware

Virus & Malware

Malicious software that harms computers without permission. Viruses and Malware self-replicate and attach to files. These threats damage data, steal information, and compromise systems. Protection requires antivirus software, updates, and cautious digital habits.

Learn More

Online Addiction

Online Addiction

Excessive use of digital devices and the internet to the point where it interferes with daily life, relationships, and emotional well-being.
