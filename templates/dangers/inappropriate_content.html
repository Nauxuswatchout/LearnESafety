What is Inappropriate Content?

Inappropriate content refers to material not suitable for children including violent content, pornography, hate speech, drug use, and other adult themes that children may stumble upon while browsing the internet or using apps.

Children can encounter inappropriate content accidentally through web searches, pop-up ads, mistyped URLs, or through content shared by others. Some content may be harmful, upsetting, or confusing for children based on their age and maturity level.

Warning Signs

  • Quickly changing screens or closing browsers when adults approach
  • Cleared browser history on a regular basis
  • Using language or making references that are not age-appropriate
  • Becoming secretive about online activities
  • Showing signs of distress after using the internet
  • Spending unusual amounts of time online, particularly late at night
  • Receiving messages or media from unknown sources
  • Changes in behavior or unusual questions about sexual or violent content

Prevention Tips

  • Use parental controls and content filters on all devices
  • Keep computers and devices in common areas of the home
  • Use child-friendly search engines like Kiddle or KidzSearch
  • Regularly check browser history and monitor online activities
  • Create separate user accounts for children with appropriate restrictions
  • Teach children to immediately close any content that makes them uncomfortable
  • Maintain open conversations about appropriate content and online safety
  • Review and approve apps before children download them
