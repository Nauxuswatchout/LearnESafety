What is Inappropriate Content?

Inappropriate content refers to material not suitable for children including violent content, pornography, hate speech, drug use, and other adult themes that children may stumble upon while browsing the internet or using apps.

Children can encounter inappropriate content accidentally through web searches, pop-up ads, mistyped URLs, or through content shared by others. Some content may be harmful, upsetting, or confusing for children based on their age and maturity level.

Important to Know

Even with safeguards in place, children may still encounter inappropriate content. Teaching them how to respond when they see something concerning is just as important as prevention.

Warning Signs

  • Quickly changing screens or closing browsers when adults approach
  • Cleared browser history on a regular basis
  • Using language or making references that are not age-appropriate
  • Becoming secretive about online activities
  • Showing signs of distress after using the internet
  • Spending unusual amounts of time online, particularly late at night
  • Receiving messages or media from unknown sources
  • Changes in behavior or unusual questions about sexual or violent content

For Parents

If you notice these signs, approach the situation with calm and open communication. Avoid accusations that might make your child reluctant to share their online experiences.

Types of Inappropriate Content

Understanding the range of content your child might encounter can help you have more specific conversations about online safety:

  • Violence and Gore - Graphic violence in videos, images, or games that can be disturbing or desensitizing
  • Sexual Content - Pornography, sexual images, or explicit discussions that are not age-appropriate
  • Hate Speech and Extremism - Content promoting discrimination, racism, or extremist views
  • Self-Harm Content - Material glorifying or providing instructions for self-harm or suicide
  • Drug and Substance Use - Content promoting or glamorizing alcohol, tobacco, or illegal drug use
  • Frightening Content - Horror content or scary challenges that can cause anxiety or nightmares

Age-Appropriate Guidelines

Content ratings exist for a reason. Following age recommendations for apps, games, and websites is an important first step in protecting children from inappropriate content.

Prevention Tips

  • Use parental controls and content filters on all devices
  • Keep computers and devices in common areas of the home
  • Use child-friendly search engines like Kiddle or KidzSearch
  • Regularly check browser history and monitor online activities
  • Create separate user accounts for children with appropriate restrictions
  • Teach children to immediately close any content that makes them uncomfortable
  • Maintain open conversations about appropriate content and online safety
  • Review and approve apps before children download them

Talking to Children About Inappropriate Content

Having regular, age-appropriate conversations about online content is essential:

  • Create a safe environment - Assure children they won't get in trouble for telling you about concerning content they've seen
  • Use age-appropriate language - Tailor your conversation to your child's level of understanding
  • Be specific but sensitive - Explain what kinds of content are inappropriate without providing unnecessary details
  • Develop a response plan - Teach children to "close and tell" when they encounter something inappropriate
  • Discuss media literacy - Help children understand that not everything they see online is real or accurate

Conversation Starter

"Sometimes people put things online that aren't good for kids to see. If you ever see anything that makes you feel uncomfortable, confused, or scared, you can always close it and come talk to me about it."
