What is Digital Addiction?

Digital addiction refers to excessive use of digital devices, social media, video games, or other online activities to the point where it interferes with daily life, relationships, and emotional well-being. It's characterized by a compulsive need to use digital technology and difficulty controlling the amount of time spent online.

Children and teenagers are particularly vulnerable to digital addiction due to their developing brains, social pressures, and the intentionally engaging design of many apps and games that incorporate reward systems to encourage continued use.

Warning Signs

  • Irritability, anxiety, or distress when unable to use devices
  • Loss of interest in offline activities previously enjoyed
  • Declining performance at school
  • Using devices to escape problems or improve mood
  • Failed attempts to cut back on screen time
  • Neglecting personal hygiene or skipping meals to stay online
  • Disrupted sleep patterns due to late-night device use
  • Lying about or hiding the amount of time spent online

Prevention Tips

  • Establish tech-free zones and times in your home
  • Create a family media use plan with clear boundaries
  • Encourage diverse interests and physical activities offline
  • Use apps to monitor and limit screen time
  • Model healthy digital habits yourself
  • Keep devices out of bedrooms at night
  • Schedule regular family activities without technology
  • Teach children mindful use of technology
