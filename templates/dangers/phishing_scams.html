Phishing & Scams

Deceptive attempts to steal personal information like usernames, passwords, location and credit card details by disguising as trustworthy entities.

What are Phishing & Online Scams?

Phishing is a type of online scam where criminals send deceptive messages designed to trick people into revealing sensitive information or installing malicious software. These attacks often disguise themselves as trustworthy entities such as banks, social media platforms, or well-known companies.

For children and teenagers, phishing can occur through fake gaming websites, social media messages, free gift offers, or even fake messages that appear to be from friends or family members.

Warning!

Never share personal information or login credentials in response to an unsolicited message, even if it appears to come from a legitimate source.

Warning Signs

  • Urgent requests for personal information
  • Poor spelling and grammar in communications
  • Offers that seem too good to be true (free games, gifts, in-game currency)
  • Links that lead to websites with URLs slightly different from legitimate sites
  • Messages asking for account credentials or personal details
  • Pop-ups or messages saying your device is infected

Safety Tip

Always check the sender's email address carefully. Legitimate organizations will use their official domain names.

Prevention Tips

  • Teach critical thinking about online content and offers
  • Install and maintain updated anti-virus and anti-malware software
  • Never click on suspicious links or download unknown attachments
  • Verify requests for information through official channels or websites
  • Use strong, unique passwords for different accounts
  • Enable two-factor authentication when available

Latest Scam Trends

Scammers are constantly evolving their tactics to exploit new technologies and trends. Recent developments include:

  • AI-generated voice scams - Using artificial intelligence to mimic the voices of family members in distress
  • QR code phishing - Placing malicious QR codes in public places that lead to fake websites
  • Investment scams - Targeting young adults with fake cryptocurrency or stock trading opportunities
  • Gaming in-app purchase scams - Offering free in-game currency or items to steal account information
