What are Online Predators?

Online predators are individuals who use the internet to identify and groom potential victims, typically through flattery, sympathy, and by offering understanding or gifts. They may pose as peers or develop a false identity to build trust.

These individuals often target vulnerable children and teenagers through social media platforms, online games, chat rooms, and messaging apps where they can establish private communications.

Important Warning

Predators often spend weeks or months building relationships with children before attempting to arrange meetings or requesting inappropriate content. This "grooming" process makes it difficult for children to recognize the danger.

Prevention Tips

  • Teach children never to share personal information online
  • Ensure privacy settings are set to the highest level on all devices and platforms
  • Discourage communication with strangers online
  • Use parental controls and monitoring software for younger children
  • Keep computers and devices in common areas of the home
  • Regularly check your child's online friends and contacts
  • Discuss the dangers of meeting online friends in person
  • Create an environment where children feel comfortable reporting concerning interactions

Warning Signs

  • Spending excessive time online, especially at night
  • Finding adult content on child's devices
  • Receiving phone calls from unfamiliar people
  • Receiving gifts, mail, or packages from unknown sources
  • Turning the computer screen off or quickly changing screens when parents enter the room
  • Becoming withdrawn from family and friends
  • Using an online account that belongs to someone else
  • Talking about "new friends" they've met online but being secretive about details

What To Do If You Suspect Your Child Is Being Targeted

  1. Stay calm - Approach the situation calmly to encourage your child to open up
  2. Preserve evidence - Take screenshots of concerning communications
  3. Report to authorities - Contact local police and the Australian Centre to Counter Child Exploitation
  4. Block contact - Remove the person from your child's contacts and block further communication
  5. Seek support - Consider counseling if your child has been affected

Remember

Reassure your child they are not in trouble. Many children hesitate to report suspicious interactions because they fear punishment or blame.

Resources for Parents and Children

  • eSafety Commissioner : Information about unwanted contact and online grooming, with reporting options.
  • Australian Centre to Counter Child Exploitation : Resources for reporting and preventing online child sexual exploitation.
  • Kids Helpline : Free, private counseling service for young people (1800 55 1800).
  • ThinkUKnow Australia : Education program focused on preventing online child sexual exploitation.
