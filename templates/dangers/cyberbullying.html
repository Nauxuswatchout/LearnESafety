Cyberbullying

warning

What is Cyberbullying?

Cyberbullying is bullying that takes place over digital devices like cell phones, computers, and tablets. It can occur through SMS, text, apps, online games, or social media platforms where people can view, participate in, or share content.

Cyberbullying includes sending, posting, or sharing negative, harmful, false, or mean content about someone else. It can include sharing personal or private information about someone else causing embarrassment or humiliation.

Warning Signs

  • Unexplained withdrawal from technology use
  • Emotional responses (tears, anger) after using devices
  • Hiding screen when others approach
  • Declining grades or loss of interest in school
  • Avoiding social situations even though they enjoyed them in the past
  • Symptoms of depression or anxiety
  • Loss of appetite, difficulty sleeping, or frequent nightmares
  • Sudden loss of friends or avoidance of social situations

Prevention Tips

  • Maintain open communication with your child
  • Monitor online activity appropriately for age
  • Teach children not to respond to cyberbullies
  • Save evidence of cyberbullying incidents
  • Report cyberbullying to school officials and online platforms
  • Discuss digital citizenship and online behavior
  • Encourage children to tell a trusted adult if they're being cyberbullied
  • Help children develop strong, supportive friendships offline
