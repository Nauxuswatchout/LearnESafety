What is Cyberbullying?

Cyberbullying is bullying that takes place over digital devices like cell phones, computers, and tablets. It can occur through SMS, text, apps, online games, or social media platforms where people can view, participate in, or share content.

Cyberbullying includes sending, posting, or sharing negative, harmful, false, or mean content about someone else. It can include sharing personal or private information about someone else causing embarrassment or humiliation.

Important to Know

Unlike traditional bullying, cyberbullying can happen 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, and can reach a child even when alone. Messages and images can be posted anonymously and distributed quickly to a wide audience.

Prevention Tips

  • Maintain open communication with your child
  • Monitor online activity appropriately for age
  • Teach children not to respond to cyberbullies
  • Save evidence of cyberbullying incidents
  • Report cyberbullying to school officials and online platforms
  • Discuss digital citizenship and online behavior
  • Encourage children to tell a trusted adult if they're being cyberbullied
  • Help children develop strong, supportive friendships offline

Warning Signs

  • Unexplained withdrawal from technology use
  • Emotional responses (tears, anger) after using devices
  • Hiding screen when others approach
  • Declining grades or loss of interest in school
  • Avoiding social situations even though they enjoyed them in the past
  • Symptoms of depression or anxiety
  • Loss of appetite, difficulty sleeping, or frequent nightmares
  • Sudden loss of friends or avoidance of social situations

Impact of Cyberbullying

The effects of cyberbullying can be far-reaching and long-lasting:

  • Emotional Impact - Victims may experience depression, anxiety, low self-esteem, and feelings of helplessness.
  • Social Impact - Children may withdraw from friends and family, avoid school, and lose interest in activities they once enjoyed.
  • Academic Impact - Studies show that bullied children often experience a decline in school performance and attendance.
  • Physical Health - Stress from bullying can lead to headaches, stomach issues, sleep problems, and other physical symptoms.

Resources for Parents and Children

  • eSafety Commissioner : Australia's official guide to online safety, with specific resources for cyberbullying.
  • Kids Helpline : Free, private and confidential 24/7 phone and online counseling service for young people aged 5 to 25.
  • Bullying. No Way! : Resources for Australian schools, parents and students to prevent and respond to bullying.
  • ReachOut Australia : Online mental health service for young people and parents, with specific resources on cyberbullying.
